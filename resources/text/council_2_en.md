"Come on, we need to gather again," you said, raising your voice above the roar of the waves.

The work around the camp slowly died down. One by one, the others came over and sat down around the fire. This time, there was something different in the air. Not just fear, not just survival. Now there was direction too.

You held the map in your hands and let your gaze move across the group before you began.

"We have both the map and the compass now. We know there is a way off this island. The question is no longer whether we can leave, but how."

For a moment, no one spoke. Everyone seemed to feel the weight of those words.

"A large raft," [person_1] said at last. "Big enough to carry all of us. If we build it sturdy enough, it should be able to take us to the mainland."

[person_2] nodded and leaned forward. "And sails. The bigger the sails, the better. If we weave them from fibers and reinforce them with leather, they should hold against the wind. With rope, we can rig everything properly."

"How big does the raft actually need to be?" [person_3] said, looking out toward the beach where the driftwood lay scattered. "At least one solid log per person, I think. Otherwise it won't have enough buoyancy. And we'll need a lot of rope to bind it all together."

You looked down at the map again and slowly traced your finger across the sea.

"I've been studying this," you said. "If I'm reading it correctly, it could take around thirty days to sail to land. That means we don't just need a raft that floats. We need food and water for everyone making the journey."

The words made several of them shift uneasily. Thirty days was a long time on the open sea.

"Then the question is..." [person_4] said quietly. "What do we do about the others? We don't know how many survivors might still be out there in the jungle. Someone could still be alive. Hungry, injured, freezing. Can we really sail away without knowing?"

No one answered right away. The fire crackled between you, and from the jungle came the distant sounds of night.

At last, you spoke.

"We build the raft," you said calmly. "We gather logs, twist rope, weave sails, and prepare provisions. We get everything ready so we can leave the island when the time comes."

You met the others' eyes, one by one.

"But we make the final decision only when everything is ready. And before then..." you nodded toward the jungle, "...maybe we should make at least one more expedition. One last proper search. If there are more people out there, then we should try to find them."

This time, there was no loud cheer. Only solemn nods and low voices.

The plan was bigger now. Heavier.

You were no longer just going to survive.

You were going to start building the way home.
