More Victims Found After Search of Island – National Minute of Silence Announced

The rescue expedition recently sent to the island where the survivors of the widely reported plane crash endured their long struggle for life has now completed its first search. The news announced today was heavy: in several locations across the island, search teams have found people who sadly did not survive.

Authorities say the discoveries confirm what many had feared since the first survivors reached the mainland after their thirty-day voyage on a self-built raft. Exactly when those found dead passed away has not yet been established, and the investigation is still ongoing. It is therefore not possible to say whether any of them were still alive when the raft left the island, or whether they had already died as a result of injuries, illness, starvation, or other severe circumstances.

Officials have made it clear that no blame can be placed on the survivors. Those who managed to escape acted under extreme pressure, with limited resources and no way of knowing how many others might still be on the island, or what condition they might be in. On the contrary, their actions are being described as an extraordinary achievement of courage, responsibility, and human endurance.

“The survivors did everything that could reasonably be asked of people in a situation no one should ever have to experience,” a spokesperson said during the afternoon. “They saved lives, organized themselves, returned into the jungle to search for others, and ultimately succeeded in bringing a group of people to safety. The fact that more victims have now been found does not change that.”

At the same time, the grief over the news is profound. For many relatives, the discoveries bring a painful end to a long period of uncertainty, hope, and fear. Identification work is continuing, and authorities are now working to contact families and provide support to loved ones.

The Prime Minister announced this evening that a national minute of silence will be held for the dead and the missing. In addition, an official memorial service is being planned to honor those who never came home. At the same time, the survivors will be recognized at a special ceremony of honor at the Prime Minister’s residence, where their strength, unity, and efforts for one another will be acknowledged.

The country therefore faces a moment of two emotions: grief for those who were lost, and gratitude for those who, against all odds, managed to survive. Together, they form a story that contains both tragedy and hope — and one that will not soon be forgotten.
