"Come on, I think we need to hold a small council," you called.

One by one, the others stopped what they were doing and gathered around the fire. The flames crackled softly in the evening air, and behind the beach the jungle lay dark and silent, as if it were listening.

"We’ve been here for a while now, and our group has grown," you began once everyone had sat down. "Several of us have talked about what we remember from the crash. Everything points to the plane having gone down somewhere inside the jungle."

You let your gaze move from face to face before continuing.

"If we’re lucky, the cockpit is there. And if the cockpit is still intact, there may also be a map and a compass. That could be our chance to actually get off this island. But I want to hear what you think."

"Yes, it would be good to leave this island," said [person_1], looking toward the black line of trees. "But the jungle is dangerous. We can’t go in there unless we can defend ourselves. We need spears."

"And we need to bring water and food," [person_2] added. "We need enough food with us to keep going."

"Fire is essential too," said [person_3]. "If we’re stuck out there overnight, we’ll need to stay warm and keep the insects away. We need to be able to make a fire."

"It’ll be hard to carry all that without something proper to pack it in," said [person_4]. "We need good backpacks, or we won’t get far."

You nodded slowly.

"Exactly. And the island is bigger than we first thought. We should probably assume this isn’t something we can solve in a single trip. We have to be careful, prepared, and think long-term. This could be the beginning of several expeditions."

For a brief moment, silence settled around the fire. Waves broke against the beach farther away, and someone nudged a glowing piece of wood with a stick.

At last, you looked at the others and asked:

"Are we agreed? Is this our next step?"

A moment later, the answers came, first a few of them, then everyone at once.

"Absolutely!"

Their voices rose together into the night, and for the first time it felt as if you were no longer just surviving on the island.

You had begun planning your way off it.
